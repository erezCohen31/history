Key Figures in the History of Israel

Theodor Herzl — Father of Modern Zionism

Theodor Herzl (1860–1904) is widely regarded as the visionary who transformed the dream of a Jewish homeland into a political movement. A journalist from Austria-Hungary, Herzl organized the First Zionist Congress in 1897, advocating for the establishment of a Jewish state in Palestine. His book, Der Judenstaat ("The Jewish State"), inspired many to support the Zionist cause.

David Ben-Gurion — Founding Father of Israel

David Ben-Gurion (1886–1973) was the primary national founder and the first Prime Minister of Israel. He proclaimed the establishment of the State of Israel on May 14, 1948, and led the country during its early, turbulent years. Ben-Gurion focused on building the country’s institutions and absorbing waves of immigrants.

Golda Meir — The Iron Lady of Israel

Golda Meir (1898–1978) was Israel’s first and only female Prime Minister to date. Known for her strong will and pragmatism, she led Israel during the challenging Yom Kippur War in 1973. Meir’s leadership helped shape Israel’s modern identity on the global stage.

Chaim Weizmann — Scientist and Statesman

Chaim Weizmann (1874–1952), a chemist and Zionist leader, played a key role in securing the Balfour Declaration of 1917, in which Britain expressed support for a Jewish homeland. Later, he became the first President of Israel.
