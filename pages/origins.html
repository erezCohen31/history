The Origins of Modern Israel

A Long-Awaited Return

After the destruction of the Second Temple in 70 CE, the Jewish people were scattered around the world. Yet for over 1,800 years, the dream of returning to their ancestral homeland remained strong. Prayers, songs, and traditions kept the hope alive — always ending with the phrase: “Next year in Jerusalem.” In the late 1800s, amid rising antisemitism in Europe, a modern movement emerged: Zionism. Led by thinkers like Theodor Herzl, Zionism called for a Jewish state in the land of Israel (then part of the Ottoman Empire). The first waves of immigrants began arriving, building communities and reclaiming land.

Did You Know?

The term "Aliyah" means "ascent" in Hebrew. It reflects not just a physical journey, but a spiritual and national rise — returning to Zion, the ancient homeland of the Jewish people.
